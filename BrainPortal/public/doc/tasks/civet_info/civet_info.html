Civet

What is CIVET?

An easy-to-use automated state-of-the-art human brain-imaging pipeline that provides:
  • Estimation of cortical thickness for understanding:
    • normal brain development throughout childhood
    • abnormal brain development in developmental disorders, such as autism
    • normal aging process of the brain
    • applications in degenerative diseases, such as Alzheimer's disease
    • asymmetry differences, gender differences
    • cortical networks
  • volumetric and corticometric functions
  • standardized optimized procedure for human brain-imaging
  • parallel processing for large populations
Advantages of Corticometry
  • Direct measure of biologically meaningful morphological features
    • cortical thickness
    • surface area
    • volume
    • folding
    • surface curvature
  • Allows longitudinal analysis of these morphological features
  • Does not confuse boundary shift with expansion/contraction
Disadvantage: no measure of sub-cortical gray

Analysis Methodologies
  • Voxel-based morphometry (VBM)
    • Tissue densities at every voxel tissue asymmetry at every voxel
  • Comparison of volumes
    • Comparison of volume whole brain, tissue type, or segmented structures
  • Cortical thickness analysis
    • Thickness at every vertex
  • Deformation Analysis (DBM)
    • Deformation difference in registration towards model

White Matter Boundary Masks
  1. Fill up ventricles - no cortex around ventricles
  2. Mask out sub-cortical gray
  3. Cut off brain stem and cerebellum
  4. Split white matter into hemispheres through corpus callosum - avoid spurious surface mesh folds
